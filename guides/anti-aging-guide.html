Introduction

Aging is a natural biological process, but premature skin aging can be managed and delayed. As the skin ages, it undergoes structural changes, losing collagen and elastin fibers at a rate of 1% per year after age 25. This guide explains how to protect your skin structure over time.

Why Skin Ages: Internal vs. External factors

Intrinsic Aging is determined by genetics and time. Over the years, cell renewal slows down, and fat pads in the cheeks naturally lose volume.

Extrinsic Aging is caused by environmental factors like UV exposure, pollution, stress, and diet. These external factors produce free radicals—unstable oxygen molecules that destroy collagen and elastin fibers.

Topical Anti-Aging Essentials

To preserve skin structure, use retinoids at night to promote cell turnover and collagen production, and apply Vitamin C and broad-spectrum sunscreen in the morning to protect against environmental damage.

Modern Non-Surgical Lifting

For sagging jowls, fine lines, or volume loss, modern medical aesthetics offer highly effective, non-surgical solutions. Injections like Botox relax expression muscles, dermal fillers restore cheek volume, and Radiofrequency (RF) skin tightening contracts loose fibers safely.
